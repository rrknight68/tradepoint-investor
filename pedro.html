The core argument
In the hold strategy, the pref investor
collects $440,000/yr while we wait 10 years
As GP partners, our big payday in the hold strategy comes entirely at Year 10 exit — not before. Meanwhile the senior pref investor collects $440,000 every single year just for holding their note. Over 10 years that's $4.4M in interest income to someone who takes no construction risk, no lease-up risk, and no market risk. We carry all the risk and wait the longest. The build-and-sell gets both of us paid in 36 months — clean, fast, and we redeploy into the next deal.

The recommendation
Sell at Month 36 — here's why it's the right move for both of us
01The GP bucket pays $4,488,546 at Month 36 in the sell strategy. That's your combined take — split between you and Robert Knight per your GP agreement — in 36 months flat. Clean close, no residual obligations.
02In the hold strategy, the pref investor earns $440,000/yr for 10 years — $4,400,000 in total interest income — while the GP bucket earns $150,256 combined over the same period. They are getting paid a salary to wait. We are not.
03The hold strategy's GP exit of $12,362,609 at Year 10 is compelling — but it requires 10 years of patience while someone else collects the annual income. At 36 months we already have the right to exit and redeploy $4,488,546 into the next deal.
04Pedro's pref tranche position recovers $917,400 at Month 36 sell close — that's his $660K ($500K invested + $160K owed) plus 13% interest for 3 years. Fully paid out, fully clean.
05The hold strategy makes sense for an OZ investor with a capital gains problem to shelter — that is not our position as GP partners. The tax benefit belongs to the outside equity investor, not to us. We get no OZ benefit from holding.
0636 months from now, we can close this deal, collect our GP split, and be underway on the next project. 10 years from now, we're still managing this asset and watching the pref investor cash checks.
